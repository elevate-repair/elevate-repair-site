Why Your Refrigerator Is Not Cooling in Lakewood

Refrigerators can stop cooling for various reasons, and understanding these can help mitigate issues in the future. One common cause is a malfunctioning thermostat, which can misread temperatures and fail to activate the cooling function. Given Lakewood’s fluctuating climate, thermostats can sometimes struggle to maintain the correct temperature.

Another reason could be dirty condenser coils. In Lakewood homes, dust and debris can accumulate more quickly due to dry conditions, causing the coils to work inefficiently. This accumulation prevents the refrigerator from dissipating heat properly, impacting its cooling capacity.

Additionally, faulty door seals can let warm air into the refrigerator, disrupting its ability to sustain a cool environment. This issue is particularly prevalent in older appliances found in many Lakewood households, where seals may have worn down over time.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Thermostat: Ensures it accurately senses the internal temperature and triggers cooling cycles.
  • Condenser Coils: Checked for dust and debris that could inhibit heat dissipation.
  • Evaporator Fan: Evaluated to ensure proper air circulation within the fridge compartment.
  • Door Seals: Inspected for any wear or gaps that might allow warm air to enter.
  • Compressor: Tested to verify it is functioning and maintaining necessary pressure levels.
  • Temperature Control Board: Analyzed to ensure it sends the correct signals to the cooling system components.

Refrigerator Repair Considerations for Lakewood Homes

Lakewood's mid-century ranch homes commonly feature galley-style kitchens where refrigerators are positioned at one end of a narrow corridor. These placements often result in less than one inch of clearance on one or both sides, and zero rear clearance in cases where the refrigerator abuts a wall directly. Restricted rear clearance prevents heat from dissipating from the condenser coils, causing the compressor to run in elevated temperature conditions that shorten its lifespan. We measure clearance and inspect coil temperature on every Lakewood refrigerator call.

Colorado's dry climate causes refrigerator door gaskets to crack and stiffen faster than in humid regions. A gasket that looks intact when inspected may leak cold air when the door is closed normally. We perform the flashlight test on every refrigerator door gasket in Lakewood as a standard diagnostic step. A failing gasket causes the compressor to run continuously and the refrigerator to struggle to hold temperature—symptoms that can be misattributed to compressor failure.

Lakewood's Bear Creek and Green Mountain neighborhoods are served by an older section of the city's electrical grid that can experience occasional voltage sag during peak summer demand. Refrigerator electronic control boards are sensitive to low voltage events, and repeated under-voltage cycles can cause boards to fail in ways that mimic compressor or sensor problems. We check voltage stability at the outlet before diagnosing control electronics on Lakewood refrigerator calls.
