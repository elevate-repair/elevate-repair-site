Why Your Dishwasher Is Leaking Water in Aurora

In Aurora, dishwashers can begin to leak for several reasons, some of which are exacerbated by the local climate and home characteristics. One common cause is a faulty door seal. Over time, the rubber gasket that keeps water inside the dishwasher can crack or become loose, allowing water to seep out. The dry climate in Aurora can sometimes accelerate this wear and tear.

Another frequent culprit is a clogged or improperly functioning drain hose. If the hose is blocked, water may not drain properly, leading to leaks. Homes in Aurora often experience hard water, which can contribute to mineral buildup, causing blockages in hoses and other components. Additionally, issues with the float switch, which controls the water level, can also lead to overfilling and subsequent leaks.

Finally, improper installation or leveling of the dishwasher can cause leaks. If your dishwasher is not level, water may pool in one area and eventually leak out. Aurora's varied home designs and foundations can sometimes mean appliances are not perfectly level, leading to these kinds of problems.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Door Seals and Gaskets — We check for cracks or wear that may allow water to escape.
  • Drain Hose — Our technicians ensure there are no blockages or leaks in the hose.
  • Float Switch — We inspect the switch to confirm it's functioning correctly to prevent overfilling.
  • Water Inlet Valve — This is examined to ensure it opens and closes properly, preventing unwanted leaks.
  • Dishwasher Pump — We check for any signs of damage or malfunction that might lead to leaks.
  • Leveling of the Appliance — Ensuring the dishwasher is properly leveled to avoid water pooling and leakage.
