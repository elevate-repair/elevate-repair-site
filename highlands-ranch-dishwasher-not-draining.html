Why Your Dishwasher Is Not Draining in Highlands Ranch

Highlands Ranch residents often face dishwasher drainage issues due to a variety of common causes. One frequent culprit is a clogged filter, which can prevent water from flowing out efficiently. This is especially prevalent in areas with hard water, common in the Highlands Ranch region, which can lead to mineral buildup.

Another possible cause is a blocked or kinked drain hose, often resulting from improper installation or movement over time. The dynamic environment of Highlands Ranch homes, with frequent use and shifting due to seasonal changes, can exacerbate these issues. Additionally, malfunctioning drain pumps or solenoids can also lead to water not evacuating properly.

Finally, the age of your appliance plays a role. Older dishwashers in Highlands Ranch homes may have worn-out components or require updates to remain efficient. Understanding these potential issues can help homeowners identify when professional repair is necessary.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Filter: Ensures it's clear of debris and allows water to pass through easily.
  • Drain Hose: Checks for kinks, clogs, or improper installation that might impede water flow.
  • Drain Pump: Verifies the pump is operational and effectively moving water out of the dishwasher.
  • Solenoid Valve: Examines the solenoid to ensure it opens and closes properly, allowing water outflow.
  • Air Gap: Inspects the air gap to prevent backflow and maintain proper drainage.
  • Float Switch: Evaluates the switch to ensure it signals the dishwasher to stop filling once the water reaches a certain level.
