Why Your Dishwasher Is Not Cleaning Dishes in Aurora

Aurora residents may encounter dishwashers that fail to clean properly due to several reasons. Hard water, prevalent in many areas of Colorado, can lead to mineral build-up that impairs functionality. This build-up can clog spray arms or the filter, reducing water flow and pressure, essential for effective cleaning.

Another common issue is the detergent itself. With the dry climate in Aurora, detergent can clump, leading to ineffective distribution during the wash cycle. Additionally, improper loading of dishes, often overlooked, can block the spray arms, preventing them from reaching all surfaces.

Temperature also plays a pivotal role; water that's not hot enough may not dissolve detergent fully, leading to residue on dishes. Many Aurora homes have water heaters set to lower temperatures to conserve energy, which might not be optimal for dishwashers.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Spray Arms: We check for clogs or damage that can obstruct water flow.
  • Filters: Our team cleans or replaces filters to ensure they’re not blocked and are functioning properly.
  • Detergent Dispenser: We ensure it opens correctly and dispenses detergent effectively.
  • Water Inlet Valve: We verify it's allowing the correct amount of water into the machine.
  • Heating Element: We test to make sure it’s heating water to the right temperature.
  • Pump and Motor Assembly: We inspect these components for any malfunctions that might affect water circulation.
