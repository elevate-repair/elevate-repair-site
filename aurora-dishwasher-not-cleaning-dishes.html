Why Your Dishwasher Is Not Cleaning Dishes in Aurora

Aurora residents may encounter dishwashers that fail to clean properly due to several reasons. Hard water, prevalent in many areas of Colorado, can lead to mineral build-up that impairs functionality. This build-up can clog spray arms or the filter, reducing water flow and pressure, essential for effective cleaning.

Another common issue is the detergent itself. With the dry climate in Aurora, detergent can clump, leading to ineffective distribution during the wash cycle. Additionally, improper loading of dishes, often overlooked, can block the spray arms, preventing them from reaching all surfaces.

Temperature also plays a pivotal role; water that's not hot enough may not dissolve detergent fully, leading to residue on dishes. Many Aurora homes have water heaters set to lower temperatures to conserve energy, which might not be optimal for dishwashers.

Dishwasher Repair Considerations for Aurora Homes

Aurora's rapid residential growth through the 2000s and 2010s brought a large number of builder-grade dishwashers into homes across the Fitzsimons, Southlands, and Saddle Rock areas. Many of these units are now hitting the 10- to 14-year mark—the period when door latches, control boards, and pump assemblies most commonly begin to fail. Our technicians are well-acquainted with the specific models installed in Aurora's tract housing developments and carry the most commonly needed parts on the truck.

Aurora's municipal water is moderately hard, which accelerates mineral deposits inside spray arms, float switch chambers, and water inlet valves. Over time this buildup mimics mechanical failure—a blocked float switch, for instance, reads the same as a faulty inlet valve. We test for mineral obstruction before replacing components, which keeps repair costs lower. Homeowners in the Aurora Highlands and Tollgate Crossing areas in particular report dishwasher issues tied directly to hard water accumulation.

At Aurora's elevation of approximately 5,471 feet, the reduced air pressure slightly affects the boiling point of water, which can influence drying performance in condensation-cycle dishwashers. If dishes seem persistently wet despite a correctly functioning heater, altitude-related calibration is worth noting to your technician during diagnosis.
