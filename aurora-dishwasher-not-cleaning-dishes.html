Why Your Dishwasher Is Not Cleaning Dishes in Aurora

Aurora residents may encounter dishwashers that fail to clean properly due to several reasons. Hard water, prevalent in many areas of Colorado, can lead to mineral build-up that impairs functionality. This build-up can clog spray arms or the filter, reducing water flow and pressure, essential for effective cleaning.

Another common issue is the detergent itself. With the dry climate in Aurora, detergent can clump, leading to ineffective distribution during the wash cycle. Additionally, improper loading of dishes, often overlooked, can block the spray arms, preventing them from reaching all surfaces.

Temperature also plays a pivotal role; water that's not hot enough may not dissolve detergent fully, leading to residue on dishes. Many Aurora homes have water heaters set to lower temperatures to conserve energy, which might not be optimal for dishwashers.

Dishwasher Repair Considerations for Aurora Homes

Spray arm clogging from mineral scale is the leading cause of cleaning failure in Aurora dishwashers. The small holes in spray arms—many under 3mm in diameter—are the first point of failure when Aurora's moderately hard water is not offset by adequate rinse aid or periodic descaling. Blocked holes reduce water coverage across the rack, especially in the back corners of the lower rack and across the upper rack. We clean and test all spray arm ports as the first diagnostic step on every Aurora not-cleaning call, which resolves the issue without part replacement in a significant share of cases.

Builder-installed dishwashers across Aurora's Fitzsimons corridor, Murphy Creek, and Saddle Rock areas were chosen for construction cost rather than wash performance. Many used simple single lower-arm spray systems with modest pump pressure. As wash pumps wear past 10 years, reduced impeller output amplifies any spray arm restriction—producing dishes with residue on the upper rack and cloudy glassware even when the machine runs a full cycle without error. We measure wash pump output pressure as part of the diagnosis to distinguish pump wear from a water supply or spray arm issue.

Low water inlet pressure is an underappreciated cause of poor cleaning in Aurora, particularly in neighborhoods near the Gun Club Road corridor and along E-470 where morning peak demand reduces residential water pressure. A dishwasher inlet receiving less than 20 PSI cannot fully pressurize the wash pump, producing the same underclean result as a worn pump motor. We test inlet pressure at the supply valve on any Aurora not-cleaning call where the pump tests within spec.
