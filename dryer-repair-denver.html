Dryer Repair Services We Offer

We service all residential dryer types, including electric dryers, gas dryers, ventless condensation dryers, and heat pump dryers. Each type uses different heating and moisture-removal methods, and our technicians understand the specific components and failure patterns of each. We stock heating elements, thermal fuses, thermostats, gas igniters, and drum belts for the most common models.

Heating system repairs are the most common dryer service we perform. In electric dryers, the heating element, high-limit thermostat, thermal fuse, and cycling thermostat work together to regulate heat. When any of these parts fails, the dryer may produce no heat at all or may overheat and shut down. In gas dryers, the gas valve coils, igniter, and flame sensor are the typical failure points. We test each component individually to confirm the exact cause before replacing anything.

Mechanical issues such as broken drum belts, worn drum rollers, damaged idler pulleys, and failed drum glides cause noise and tumbling problems. A snapped belt stops the drum entirely, while worn rollers and glides create thumping or squealing sounds. We replace these wear parts to restore quiet, reliable operation. For units with a damaged drum bearing or support shaft, we can rebuild the drum assembly rather than replacing the entire dryer.

Airflow restrictions are a leading cause of long drying times and overheating. A clogged lint screen, blocked exhaust vent, or crushed vent hose forces the dryer to work harder and longer. We inspect the complete airflow path from the lint trap through the exhaust vent termination and clear any blockages. We service all major dryer brands including Whirlpool, Maytag, Samsung, LG, GE, Frigidaire, Bosch, Electrolux, Amana, and Speed Queen.
