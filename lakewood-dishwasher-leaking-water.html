Why Your Dishwasher Is Leaking Water in Lakewood

Lakewood homeowners often encounter dishwasher leaks, which can be particularly concerning due to potential water damage. One common cause is a faulty door gasket, which can crack or wear out over time, especially with the fluctuating temperatures experienced in Colorado. When these gaskets fail, water escapes during the wash cycle, leading to annoying puddles on your kitchen floor.

Another possible culprit is a clogged or damaged drain hose. Over time, debris can build up, blocking the hose and causing water to back up and overflow. Additionally, the hard water typical in Lakewood can lead to mineral deposits, which may exacerbate these blockages. Lastly, improper leveling of the dishwasher can also result in leaks, as water might pool unevenly and spill out.

Furthermore, issues with the dishwasher's float switch or inlet valve can lead to overfilling, causing leaks. These components regulate the water level inside the dishwasher, and any malfunction can cause it to fill beyond capacity. Given Lakewood's varied housing styles, installations might vary, necessitating professional evaluation to diagnose and solve these leaking issues effectively.

Dishwasher Repair Considerations for Lakewood Homes

Lakewood's housing stock spans from 1950s ranch homes in South Lakewood and the Green Gables area to newer construction near Belmar and the Colfax corridor. The city's older home sections frequently have dishwashers connected to aging braided supply hoses installed during kitchen remodels 20–30 years ago. These hoses are a common source of slow leaks that pool under the dishwasher, and we inspect all connection points—not just the appliance—before identifying the source of every leak call.

Lakewood's water supply is moderately hard, and mineral scale from this supply accelerates the deterioration of rubber door gaskets, inlet hose connections, and dispenser fittings. In homes where the dishwasher has been in service for more than a decade, scale-accelerated seal degradation is often a contributing factor in leak calls alongside the primary mechanical source. We test water-facing seals and supply connections as part of every leaking dishwasher diagnosis in Lakewood.

Many mid-century Lakewood homes have undergone kitchen renovations where dishwashers were added to originally dishwasher-free layouts. In these cases, the supply line is often routed from a nearby shutoff under the sink and may be undersized or connected with adapters that are now decades old. We replace supply connections as part of leak repairs when the line condition warrants it, rather than simply repairing the appliance.
