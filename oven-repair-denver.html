Oven Repair Services We Offer

We repair freestanding ranges, slide-in ranges, wall ovens, double ovens, and cooktops in both gas and electric configurations. Oven repair requires precise work with heating systems and temperature controls, and our technicians are trained to handle both safely. We stock bake elements, broil elements, gas igniters, oven sensors, and control boards for the most common brands and models.

Heating failures in electric ovens are usually caused by a burned-out bake or broil element, a faulty oven temperature sensor, or a defective control board. The bake element is located at the bottom of the oven cavity and handles most cooking, while the broil element at the top provides high direct heat. We test element continuity and sensor resistance to determine which component has failed and replace it. In gas ovens, a weak or cracked igniter is the most common cause of no-heat problems. The igniter must reach a specific temperature to open the gas valve, and when it weakens with age, it can no longer do so reliably.

Temperature accuracy problems affect baking and roasting results. An oven temperature sensor that reads incorrectly will cause the control board to regulate heat to the wrong level. A failing gas valve or partially burned-out element can also cause temperature inconsistencies. We measure actual oven temperature against the set temperature and replace the sensor, element, or valve as needed to restore accuracy.

Stovetop burner repairs cover both gas and electric configurations. Gas burners may fail to ignite due to a faulty spark module, clogged burner ports, or a defective spark electrode. Electric coil burners rely on the element receptacle and infinite switch, while smooth-top electric ranges use radiant elements controlled by switches or a glass-top control board. We diagnose and repair all stovetop issues. All oven and range brands are serviced, including GE, Whirlpool, Samsung, LG, Frigidaire, KitchenAid, Maytag, Bosch, Viking, and Wolf.
