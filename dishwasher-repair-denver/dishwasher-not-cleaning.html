Why This Happens

A dishwasher that leaves food stuck to plates, glasses with a cloudy film, or silverware still coated in grease is one of the most common complaints we handle. The root cause usually traces back to water delivery or mechanical wash issues. Clogged spray arms are a frequent culprit: over time, mineral deposits from Denver's hard water and small food particles block the tiny spray holes, reducing the water pressure needed to blast dishes clean. When the spray arms can't rotate freely or deliver adequate water coverage, entire sections of the rack come out dirty.

A worn wash impeller is another likely cause. The impeller sits inside the wash pump and forces water up through the spray arms at high pressure. When its fins wear down or crack, the pump can't generate enough force to clean effectively. Similarly, a defective water inlet valve may not open fully, starving the dishwasher of the water volume it needs. If the tub doesn't fill to the proper level, the spray arms can't do their job regardless of their condition.

Low water temperature also plays a major role. Dishwasher detergent is formulated to activate at around 120 degrees Fahrenheit. If the incoming water is too cool or the dishwasher's internal heating element has failed, grease and food residue won't dissolve properly. Finally, a broken detergent dispenser that doesn't pop open at the right time during the wash cycle means your detergent sits trapped in the door, never reaching your dishes when it matters most.

Signs You Need Professional Repair

  • Food particles remain on dishes, pots, or utensils after a complete cycle
  • Glasses come out cloudy, filmy, or with white residue spots
  • The detergent pod or tablet is still intact or partially dissolved after the cycle
  • The spray arms don't spin freely when you rotate them by hand
  • Water level inside the tub appears lower than normal during the wash cycle

How We Fix It

Our technician starts with a full diagnostic of the wash system. We inspect the spray arms for clogs and damage, test the wash motor and impeller for proper output, and measure the water inlet valve flow rate. We also check the water temperature reaching the dishwasher, the detergent dispenser mechanism, and the cycle timer to ensure each phase runs for the correct duration. This systematic approach lets us pinpoint the exact failure rather than guessing.

Once we identify the problem, we carry out the repair on the spot. Whether it's clearing mineral buildup from spray arms, replacing a worn impeller, installing a new water inlet valve, or fixing the detergent dispenser latch, we stock common parts for most major dishwasher brands on our service vehicles.
