Dishwasher Leaking Water in Denver

Water pooling under or around your dishwasher can damage flooring and cabinetry fast. Our technicians locate the source of the leak and repair it the same day to protect your home.

Why This Happens

Dishwasher leaks can originate from several different points, and the location of the water on your floor often tells us where to look first. The most common cause is a worn door gasket. The rubber or vinyl seal that runs along the perimeter of the door compresses every time you close it, and after years of use, it can crack, harden, or develop flat spots that no longer form a watertight seal. When the gasket fails, water seeps out around the door during the wash and rinse cycles, typically pooling directly in front of the unit.

Leaks from underneath the dishwasher usually point to a damaged tub seal or loose hose connections. The main tub seal sits where the wash motor mounts to the bottom of the tub. Over time, this seal deteriorates from heat and detergent exposure, allowing water to drip down around the motor housing. Hose clamps on the drain hose, recirculation hose, and water inlet line can also work loose from vibration, creating slow drips that you might not notice until water damage becomes visible on adjacent flooring or cabinets.

A cracked spray arm can also cause leaking by directing water jets sideways against the door seal rather than down onto the dishes, forcing water past the gasket. The float switch, which monitors the water level inside the tub, can also be responsible. If the float gets stuck in the down position due to debris or a failed float switch, the dishwasher overfills and water spills over the edge of the tub. Additionally, a worn or misaligned door latch that doesn't pull the door tight against the gasket can create gaps that allow water to escape during high-pressure wash cycles.

Signs You Need Professional Repair

  • Water puddles on the floor in front of or beneath the dishwasher after running a cycle
  • Visible warping, staining, or soft spots on the flooring near the dishwasher
  • The door gasket appears cracked, brittle, or no longer sits flush against the tub
  • Water drips from underneath the unit during the wash cycle
  • A musty smell around the dishwasher suggesting hidden moisture accumulation

How We Fix It

Our technician runs the dishwasher through a cycle while carefully inspecting every potential leak point. We examine the door gasket for wear and compression loss, check the tub seal around the motor mount, and inspect every hose connection and clamp underneath the unit. We also verify the float switch operates correctly, test the water inlet valve for drips, and examine the spray arms for cracks or damage that could misdirect water flow. By watching the machine run, we can see exactly where water escapes.

Once the source is identified, we perform the repair immediately. Common fixes include replacing the door gasket, installing a new tub seal, tightening or replacing hose clamps, swapping out a cracked spray arm, or correcting a stuck float switch. We also check the door latch alignment to ensure proper closure pressure against the seal. Our diagnostic visit is $89, which applies toward any repair you approve.
