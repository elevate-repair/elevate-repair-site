Dishwasher Making Noise Repair in Denver

Grinding, buzzing, or humming sounds from your dishwasher usually signal a mechanical problem that will get worse over time. Our technicians identify the source and fix it the same day.

Why This Happens

A dishwasher produces some normal operational sounds, including the hum of the wash motor, the swish of water through the spray arms, and the click of the detergent dispenser opening. But when new, loud, or unusual noises appear, they almost always indicate a mechanical component that is wearing out or has already failed. The most common source of grinding or growling sounds is worn wash motor bearings. The wash motor runs continuously throughout the wash and rinse cycles, spinning at high speed to drive the pump and spray arms. As its bearings deteriorate, metal-on-metal contact produces a grinding noise that grows louder over time. If left unaddressed, the motor can seize completely, stopping the dishwasher mid-cycle.

A defective drain pump is another frequent noise source, typically producing a loud buzzing or humming during the drain phase. The drain pump motor can develop worn bearings of its own, or its impeller can crack, causing it to wobble and strike the pump housing. A broken spray arm bearing creates a rhythmic scraping or squealing sound as the spray arm rotates unevenly on its mount. When the bearing wears down, the spray arm drops slightly and can drag against the dish racks or the tub floor, producing an unmistakable scraping noise with each revolution.

Debris caught in the chopper blade assembly is a common cause of sudden grinding or crunching noises. The chopper blade sits in the pump area and is designed to break down food particles small enough to pass through the drain. When hard objects like broken glass, bones, fruit pits, or small utensils slip past the filter and reach the chopper, they produce loud, alarming sounds. Loose mounting brackets can also cause rattling and vibration noises, especially during the drain cycle when the pump creates more vibration than the wash motor. Over time, the screws and clips that secure the dishwasher to the underside of the countertop or to the cabinet sides can work loose, allowing the entire unit to vibrate against surrounding surfaces.

Signs You Need Professional Repair

  • A grinding or growling sound during the wash cycle that has gotten progressively louder
  • A loud buzzing or humming noise during the drain phase at the end of the cycle
  • A rhythmic scraping or squealing sound that occurs in a repeating pattern
  • A sudden crunching or rattling noise that started after running a load
  • Vibration or banging sounds that seem to come from the dishwasher housing or surrounding cabinetry

How We Fix It

Our technician runs the dishwasher through its cycles while listening carefully to isolate the noise source. Different sounds point to different components: grinding during the wash phase typically indicates wash motor bearings, buzzing during drain points to the drain pump, and scraping in a rhythmic pattern suggests a spray arm bearing issue. We remove the lower spray arm and filter assembly to inspect the chopper blade area for foreign objects and debris. We also check the mounting brackets and hardware that secure the dishwasher in its cabinet opening to identify any looseness causing vibration noise.

Depending on the diagnosis, repairs may include replacing the wash motor or its bearing assembly, installing a new drain pump, replacing a worn spray arm and its bearing ring, clearing debris from the chopper blade area, or re-securing the mounting brackets and leveling legs.
