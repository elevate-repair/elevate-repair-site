Ice Maker Not Making Ice Repair in Denver

An ice maker that stops producing ice is one of the most common refrigerator complaints. Elevate Repair provides same-day ice maker diagnosis and repair throughout Denver for all refrigerator brands.

Why This Happens

Ice makers depend on a reliable water supply, consistent freezer temperatures, and a properly functioning mechanical cycle to produce ice. The water inlet valve must open at the right time to fill the ice mold with the correct amount of water. If the valve is defective, has insufficient water pressure, or is blocked by mineral buildup, the mold receives no water and no ice is produced. A household water pressure below 20 psi will prevent the valve from opening fully.

Freezer temperature plays a critical role in ice production. The freezer must maintain a temperature between 0 and 5 degrees Fahrenheit for the ice maker to cycle properly. If the freezer is too warm due to a defrost system problem, a faulty evaporator fan, or a dirty condenser, the ice maker will not initiate a harvest cycle because the thermostat built into the ice maker module senses that the mold has not reached the required temperature.

The ice maker module itself can fail mechanically or electrically. The module contains a small motor that rotates the ejector arms to push ice cubes out of the mold and into the bin. A burned-out motor, a failed mold thermostat, or a faulty control board within the module will stop the cycle entirely. In some models, the optical or mechanical ice level sensor can malfunction and tell the ice maker that the bin is full when it is actually empty, preventing new ice from being made.

Signs You Need Professional Repair

  • Ice bin is empty and no new ice has been produced in 24 hours
  • Ice cubes are unusually small, thin, or hollow
  • Ice maker is making a clicking or buzzing sound but producing no ice
  • Water is leaking into the ice bin or onto the freezer floor
  • Ice has a bad taste or odor despite a recently replaced water filter

How We Fix It

Our technician checks the water supply line for kinks or shutoff valve issues, tests the water inlet valve for proper voltage and flow, and measures the freezer temperature to confirm it is within the correct range. We inspect the ice maker module for mechanical function, test the mold thermostat, and verify that the ice level sensor is reading correctly.

Common parts replaced include the water inlet valve, ice maker module assembly, mold thermostat, water filter, ice level sensor, and water supply line. If the ice maker assembly is beyond repair, we install a new one compatible with your refrigerator model. Most ice maker repairs are completed in under one hour.
