Fridge Leaking Water

Water pooling under or inside your refrigerator can damage your floors and indicate a problem that will worsen over time. Elevate Repair provides same-day leak diagnosis and repair for refrigerators in Denver.

Why This Happens

The most common cause of a leaking refrigerator is a clogged defrost drain. During each defrost cycle, melted frost drains through a small tube at the bottom of the freezer compartment into a drain pan beneath the unit, where it evaporates. If this drain becomes blocked by ice, food debris, or mineral deposits, the water backs up into the freezer floor and eventually overflows into the refrigerator compartment or onto your kitchen floor. This is especially common in units that have been running for several years without maintenance.

A cracked or overflowing drain pan is another potential source. The drain pan sits underneath the refrigerator near the compressor and condenser fan. Under normal conditions, the heat from these components evaporates the water before the pan fills up. However, if the pan is cracked, improperly positioned, or if the unit is producing more condensation than usual due to high humidity or a door seal issue, water can overflow onto the floor. A failed condenser fan can also reduce evaporation, causing the pan to fill faster than normal.

Water supply line leaks affect refrigerators equipped with ice makers and water dispensers. The supply line connects to the water inlet valve at the back of the unit, and any loose connection, cracked fitting, or deteriorated supply line can produce a slow or steady leak. Door gasket problems allow warm, humid air to enter the refrigerator, creating excess condensation that collects on interior surfaces and drips onto shelves or the floor below.

Signs You Need Professional Repair

  • Puddles of water appearing on the floor in front of or under the refrigerator
  • Water pooling at the bottom of the refrigerator compartment or in the crisper drawers
  • Ice forming at the bottom of the freezer compartment
  • Water stains or warping on the floor beneath the refrigerator
  • Dripping sounds inside the unit that were not present before

How We Fix It

Our technician identifies the source of the leak by inspecting the defrost drain, drain pan, water supply line, water inlet valve, and door gaskets. For a clogged defrost drain, we clear the blockage using warm water and a flexible tool, then verify that water flows freely to the drain pan. If the drain line has a history of repeated clogs, we may install a drain heater clip to prevent future ice buildup.

For supply line leaks, we tighten or replace fittings and inspect the line for cracks or wear. Cracked drain pans are replaced entirely. Worn door gaskets are replaced to restore a proper seal and eliminate condensation issues. Common parts replaced include the defrost drain assembly, drain pan, water supply line, water inlet valve, and refrigerator door gasket. Most leak repairs are completed within one hour.
