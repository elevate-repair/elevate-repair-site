Washer Repair Services We Offer

We repair all types of residential washing machines, including top-load agitator models, top-load high-efficiency impeller machines, and front-load washers. Each design has its own set of common failure points, and our technicians are trained to work on all of them. We carry drain pumps, lid switches, door latches, water inlet valves, and drive belts on our service vehicles for same-visit repairs.

Drain and spin problems are the most frequent washer issues we encounter. A clogged drain pump, failed pump motor, or blocked drain hose can leave water sitting in the tub. On the spin side, a worn drive coupler, broken motor coupling, or failed lid switch can prevent the drum from reaching spin speed. Front-load washers may develop door lock or door boot seal issues that prevent proper operation. We test each component systematically to identify the root cause before recommending a repair.

Control board and timer failures have become more common as washers have added electronic features. A faulty main control board, user interface board, or pressure switch can cause erratic behavior, error codes, or a completely unresponsive machine. We diagnose electronic faults using manufacturer-specific error codes and multimeter testing to confirm which board or sensor needs replacement.

Bearing and suspension repairs address noise and vibration issues. Worn tub bearings produce a loud rumbling during the spin cycle, while damaged shock absorbers or suspension springs allow excessive drum movement. We replace bearings, seals, shocks, springs, and counterweights to restore smooth, quiet operation. All washer brands are serviced, including Whirlpool, Maytag, Samsung, LG, GE, Frigidaire, Bosch, Electrolux, Amana, and Speed Queen. Common washer problems we fix include washer not draining, washer not spinning, and washer leaking water.
