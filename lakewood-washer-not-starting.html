Why Your Washer Is Not Starting in Lakewood

Living in Lakewood, residents might face unique challenges with their washers, such as the appliance not starting. This issue can be attributed to several common causes. One frequent culprit is a malfunctioning power supply. Given the diverse weather conditions in Lakewood, from sunny days to unexpected storms, power surges or outages can disrupt your washer's ability to power on.

Another potential cause is a faulty lid switch. If the washer's lid is not properly closed or the switch is damaged, the machine's safety mechanism will prevent it from starting. In homes where laundry loads are frequent, wear and tear on this component can be more pronounced. Additionally, Lakewood's varying water quality can lead to mineral build-up in the washer, impacting its internal sensors and starting mechanisms.

A third consideration is the control board. This critical component manages the washer's functions, and if it fails, the washer may not start. With Lakewood's varying temperatures, electronic components can sometimes be more susceptible to failure, necessitating professional assessment and repair.

Washer Repair Considerations for Lakewood Homes

Washer not-starting complaints in Lakewood's mid-century ranch homes frequently involve control board power supply failures caused by the home's electrical characteristics. Lakewood's Bear Creek and Green Mountain neighborhoods are served by an older section of the city's electrical distribution grid that can experience brief voltage sags during peak demand periods. Modern washer control boards are sensitive to undervoltage events, and a board that has experienced repeated low-voltage cycles may develop a startup fault that prevents cycle initiation even when outlet voltage appears normal during testing. We check outlet voltage under load and inspect the control board for visible burn marks before assuming a mechanical cause on Lakewood not-starting calls.

Lid switch failure in top-load washers is the most common single-component cause of not-starting in Lakewood's older machine population. Lakewood homes built in the 1960s and 1970s often have laundry areas in basement utility rooms where humidity levels cycle with the seasons—humid in summer, very dry in winter—causing the plastic lid switch housing to contract and expand repeatedly until the switch actuator cracks. A top-load Lakewood washer that powers on, accepts a cycle selection, but produces no motor response when the start button is pressed almost always has a failed lid switch. We test lid switch continuity as a baseline step before any further disassembly.

Front-load washers installed in Lakewood basement utility hallways during the 2000s remodel period are now reaching ages where door latch assemblies commonly fail. The door latch solenoid must confirm a locked door to the control board before any cycle can begin; a solenoid that has worn out of alignment in a narrow basement hallway—where the washer door can only partially open due to space constraints—often shows premature latch wear from not being fully opened between cycles. A front-load Lakewood washer that displays a door lock error on every start attempt has a mechanical latch problem more often than an electronic control failure.
