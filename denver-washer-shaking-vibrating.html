Why Your Washer Is Shaking Vibrating in Denver

One common reason washers shake or vibrate excessively is an uneven load. If you are washing a bulky item like a comforter, it might not distribute evenly, causing the machine to become unbalanced. This is especially relevant in Denver, where larger loads or heavy blankets might be necessary during cold winters.

Another cause can be related to the floor on which the washer is placed. Homes in Denver can have uneven flooring due to the shifting and settling that occurs over time. This can lead to inadequate stability, causing the washer to vibrate more than usual.

Additionally, a damaged shock absorber or suspension spring can lead to a vibrating washer. These components help dampen the movement of the drum; if they are worn out, vibrations increase significantly. In Denver's diverse home constructions, older properties may especially experience these issues.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Leveling Feet — Ensures the washer is stable on Denver’s often uneven floors.
  • Suspension Springs — Checks for wear that could lead to uncontrolled vibrations.
  • Shock Absorbers — Inspects these to reduce excessive drum movement.
  • Drum Bearings — Evaluates their condition to prevent rough spinning.
  • Drive Belt — Examines for any wear that might cause imbalance.
  • Balance Ring — Assesses its functionality in maintaining balance during cycles.

Common Questions About Washer Shaking Vibrating Issues

Why does my washer shake violently during spin cycles? This could be due to an unbalanced load, worn suspension springs, or uneven flooring. Our technicians can accurately diagnose this during a service call in Denver.

Is it safe to use my washer if it’s vibrating? While it might seem manageable, continued use can lead to more significant damage to the washer or its surroundings. It's best to have it inspected by professionals like those at Elevate Repair.
