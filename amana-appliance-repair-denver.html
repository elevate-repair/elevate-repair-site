Common Amana Repair Issues

Amana bottom-freezer refrigerators commonly develop drain clogs that cause water to pool at the bottom of the refrigerator compartment or leak onto the kitchen floor. During the automatic defrost cycle, the ice that melts off the evaporator coils is supposed to flow through a drain tube into a pan underneath the unit. When this drain tube becomes clogged with ice, food particles, or mineral deposits, the water backs up and overflows into the fresh food section. In Denver, where water hardness is moderate, mineral buildup in the drain tube accelerates this problem. We clear the drain, flush the tube, and apply a preventive treatment to reduce future clogs.

Amana top-load washers are among the most straightforward washing machines to repair, but they do have a consistent failure point in the lid switch assembly. The lid switch is a safety device that prevents the washer from spinning while the lid is open. When it fails, the washer will fill with water and agitate but will not advance to the spin cycle, leaving clothes soaking wet at the end of the wash. In some Amana models, the lid switch failure also prevents the washer from draining. We replace the lid switch and test the entire wash cycle to confirm normal operation.

Amana appliances use basic mechanical and electromechanical controls in many of their models rather than the complex electronic control boards found in premium brands. While this makes them more affordable and often simpler to repair, the thermostat and temperature control components in these basic models can drift or fail over time. In Amana ovens and ranges, an out-of-calibration thermostat may cause the oven to run too hot or too cold relative to the set temperature. In Amana refrigerators, a failing cold control thermostat may cause the unit to run continuously or not run at all. We test and replace thermostats for all Amana cooking and cooling appliances.

Amana dryers share the same platform as Whirlpool dryers, and common issues include worn drum rollers that produce a thumping or rumbling noise, failed heating elements on electric models, and gas valve coil failures on gas models. Amana dishwashers may experience water inlet valve failures or pump motor issues. Because Amana parts are widely available and cross-compatible with Whirlpool, we can typically complete most Amana repairs on the first visit without needing to order specialty parts.
