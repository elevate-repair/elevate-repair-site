Why Your Washer Is Not Draining in Lakewood

In Lakewood, a common issue with washers is their failure to drain properly, causing frustration and inconvenience. This problem can arise from several causes, many of which are influenced by local conditions. For instance, the mineral-rich water in the area can lead to buildup in hoses and pumps, hindering proper drainage over time.

Another frequent culprit is a clogged drain hose. Lakewood homes, especially those located in older neighborhoods, may have plumbing that contributes to blockages. Foreign objects such as coins, buttons, or even detergent buildup can obstruct the hose, preventing water from exiting the washer.

Sometimes the issue lies with the washer's pump. Wear and tear on the pump can occur due to the heavy use often required in family households around Lakewood. The pump might fail, causing water to remain in the drum after the cycle completes. Addressing these issues promptly is essential to maintain your appliance's performance.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Drain Hose — Our technicians check for any blockages that might be preventing water from flowing out.
  • Drain Pump — We inspect the pump for signs of wear or any obstructions that could impede its function.
  • Door Lock — A malfunctioning door lock can halt the draining process, so we ensure it's working correctly.
  • Water Level Control — This component helps regulate the water flow and is checked for accuracy.
  • Belt — A worn or broken belt can stop the washer drum from spinning, affecting drainage.
  • Electrical Connections — We verify that all electrical connections are secure and functioning properly.
