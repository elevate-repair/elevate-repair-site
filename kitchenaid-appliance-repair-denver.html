Common KitchenAid Repair Issues

KitchenAid dishwashers are among the most popular in their price range, and the most common repair involves the upper wash arm and spray assembly. Over time, the check ball in the diverter motor can wear out, directing all water pressure to the lower spray arm and leaving the top rack with poor cleaning results. The Clean Water Wash filtration system also requires periodic maintenance. If the filter is not cleaned, food particles recirculate and redeposit on dishes. On older KitchenAid Superba dishwashers, the control board is a known failure point, causing the unit to stop mid-cycle or display blinking lights without responding to button presses.

KitchenAid refrigerators, both freestanding and built-in models, can develop compressor relay or start device failures. The symptoms include a clicking sound every few minutes as the compressor attempts to start and then shuts off on overload. In built-in KitchenAid refrigerators, condenser coils are located behind the unit and need periodic cleaning. Denver's dry, dusty climate can accelerate condenser coil buildup, reducing cooling efficiency and putting strain on the compressor. We clean condensers and replace failed compressor components to restore proper cooling.

KitchenAid gas ranges and wall ovens use the Even-Heat True Convection system with a bow-tie shaped heating element and fan. When the convection fan motor fails, the oven may still heat but will produce uneven results, with one side of the oven significantly hotter than the other. The flat igniter on gas models is the same design used across the Whirlpool family and is the most frequently replaced component in KitchenAid gas ovens. Dual fuel models, which combine a gas cooktop with an electric oven, may experience issues with either system independently.

KitchenAid cooktops with sealed gas burners can develop igniter electrode issues where one or more burners click continuously without lighting, or conversely, refuse to spark at all. The spark module that controls all burners can fail, or individual igniters can crack from spills and cleaning product exposure. Induction KitchenAid cooktops may display error codes when the power board fails to communicate properly with the individual element controls. We diagnose and repair both gas and electric KitchenAid cooktop configurations.
