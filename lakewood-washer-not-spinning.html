Why Your Washer Is Not Spinning in Lakewood

Washers can encounter several issues that prevent them from spinning properly, especially given the varied climate conditions in Lakewood. One common cause is an unbalanced load, which can occur when clothes settle on one side of the drum, making it difficult for the washer to spin. This is particularly problematic in homes with older models that lack advanced balancing technology.

Another reason could be a faulty lid switch. This safety feature prevents the washer from spinning when the lid is open. If the switch is defective or misaligned, it may not register when the lid is closed, stopping the spin cycle. Additionally, the wear and tear on the drive belt due to Lakewood’s dry climate can cause it to snap or slip, preventing the drum from spinning.

In some cases, a malfunctioning motor or a broken motor coupling could be the culprit. These components are essential for transferring power to the drum. The mineral-rich water in Lakewood can contribute to the faster deterioration of these parts, leading to spin issues. Understanding these common problems can help homeowners in Lakewood know when to call for professional help.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Lid Switch Assembly: Ensures the lid is closed properly to enable the spin cycle.
  • Drive Belt: Checks for wear or damage that might prevent the drum from spinning.
  • Motor Coupling: Evaluates the connection between the motor and transmission for any breaks.
  • Clutch: Inspects for functionality to help the washer achieve the necessary spin speed.
  • Drive Motor: Tests if the motor is in good working condition and can power the spin cycle.
  • Control Board: Verifies if the board is sending the correct signals for the spin cycle to activate.
