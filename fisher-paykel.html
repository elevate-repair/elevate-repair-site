Common Fisher & Paykel Appliance Issues

The Fisher & Paykel DishDrawer is the most common product we service from this brand. The drawer mechanism uses a motorized rail system that can develop alignment issues or motor failures over time, causing the drawer to stick, not open fully, or fail to latch. Water leak issues around the drawer seal are also common, particularly after several years of use. The electronic control module in DishDrawer units can display fault codes that require specific diagnostic procedures to interpret correctly.

Fisher & Paykel refrigerators with ActiveSmart technology use variable-speed compressors and adaptive defrost systems. When the main control board fails, the refrigerator may run continuously, not cool at all, or cycle erratically. Their washers with SmartDrive direct-drive motors eliminate the belt and pulley system used by most brands, but the rotor position sensor and stator can fail, causing the unit to stop mid-cycle or display error codes. We carry the diagnostic tools needed to properly test these systems.
