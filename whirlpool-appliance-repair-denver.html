Common Whirlpool Repair Issues

Whirlpool top-load washers, particularly the Cabrio series (now branded as the standard HE top-loader), are prone to bearing and tub seal failures. When the bearings wear out, you will notice a loud rumbling or grinding during the spin cycle. This is a labor-intensive repair because the outer tub must be separated, but it is almost always more cost-effective than replacing the machine. Our technicians carry the bearing and seal kits needed for same-day completion on most Cabrio models.

Whirlpool refrigerators frequently develop ice maker failures, especially in the WRF and WRS French door and side-by-side models. The ice maker module itself may stop cycling, or the inlet valve may restrict water flow due to mineral buildup. In some units, the defrost drain freezes over, causing water to pool beneath the deli drawer or leak onto the floor. We diagnose and resolve all of these issues, including clearing frozen drain lines and replacing faulty inlet valves.

The Whirlpool Duet front-load washer line has a documented history of door boot seal deterioration. The rubber gasket around the door develops mold or tears, leading to leaks during the wash cycle. Replacing the boot seal requires careful disassembly of the front panel and door assembly. We also see control board failures in older Duet models where the unit will not start or displays error codes such as F01 or F21.

Whirlpool dishwashers commonly experience issues with the door latch assembly and the control board. When the latch fails, the dishwasher will not start or may stop mid-cycle. The electronic control boards in models from the mid-2010s can develop solder joint cracks that cause intermittent failures. Whirlpool dryers are generally reliable, but the most frequent repair involves a worn drum roller or broken belt, both of which cause thumping noises or prevent the drum from turning.

Whirlpool gas and electric ranges may develop igniter failures on gas models or bake element burnouts on electric models. The flat igniter used in many Whirlpool gas ovens draws current to open the gas valve, and as it weakens over time, the oven may take longer to preheat or fail to light entirely. Replacing the igniter is a straightforward repair that restores full function.
