Why Your Refrigerator Is Not Cooling in Denver

Refrigerators in Denver can face a variety of issues that lead to cooling problems. One common cause is a malfunctioning thermostat, which may fail due to temperature fluctuations typical of Denver's climate. A faulty thermostat cannot properly regulate the refrigerator's cooling cycles, leading to inefficient performance.

Another frequent issue is a dirty condenser coil. Dust and debris can accumulate on the coil, especially in areas like Denver where homes might use forced air heating systems that circulate particles. This buildup forces the refrigerator to work harder to maintain its temperature, eventually causing cooling issues.

Additionally, a broken door seal might be the culprit. In older Denver homes, wear and tear over time can compromise the door gasket, allowing warm air to enter and reducing the fridge’s ability to keep contents cool. Ensuring the gasket is intact is crucial for maintaining optimal refrigeration.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Thermostat: We check if the thermostat is accurately reading and maintaining the correct temperature.
  • Condenser Coils: The coils are inspected and cleaned to ensure they are free of dust and debris.
  • Evaporator Fan: We ensure the fan is operational, as it circulates the air to cool the fridge evenly.
  • Door Seals: Our technicians examine the door seals for any cracks or gaps that could let warm air in.
  • Compressor: We assess the compressor for any signs of wear that could affect cooling.
  • Refrigerant Levels: We check for refrigerant leaks and ensure levels are adequate for cooling.
