Why Your Washer Is Not Filling in Aurora

In Aurora, washers failing to fill can be attributed to several common issues. The water inlet valve is often a culprit, especially if mineral buildup from local water sources clogs the valve. This blockage can prevent water from entering the machine, leading to an incomplete cycle. Additionally, a malfunctioning lid switch can be a problem, as it signals the washer to stop filling.

Another potential cause is a kinked or blocked hose. With Aurora's seasonal changes, hoses can become brittle and crack or kink, restricting water flow. The climate can also affect washers, where extreme temperatures may cause components like hoses to degrade faster than expected. It's important to regularly check and maintain these parts to ensure optimal performance.

Lastly, water pressure issues in Aurora homes can lead to filling problems. If the water pressure is too low, your washer may not fill as it should. Having an experienced technician assess your plumbing can help determine if this is contributing to your appliance's issues.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Water Inlet Valve — Ensures it is free of debris and functioning correctly.
  • Lid Switch — Verifies it's properly signaling the washer to fill.
  • Hoses — Checks for kinks, cracks, or blockages that impede water flow.
  • Pressure Switch — Assesses if it's accurately detecting water levels.
  • Control Board — Evaluates for any electronic malfunctions affecting the fill cycle.
  • Plumbing Connections — Inspects for adequate water pressure and secure connections.
