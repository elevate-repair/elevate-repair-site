Why Your Refrigerator Is Leaking Water in Arvada

Refrigerators can leak water for several reasons, and understanding the underlying cause is crucial to effectively addressing the issue. In Arvada, the fluctuating temperatures and varying humidity levels can sometimes exacerbate these problems. One common cause of a leaking refrigerator is a blocked defrost drain. This blockage can lead to water accumulation and eventual leakage onto your kitchen floor.

Another typical culprit is a broken or improperly installed water filter. If a filter is not correctly seated, it may cause water to leak. Moreover, the water inlet valve, which supplies water to the refrigerator, might malfunction due to mineral deposits, a common issue in areas with hard water like Arvada. Such deposits can cause the valve to remain open, leading to leaks.

Additionally, the drain pan, which collects condensation, may have cracks or be improperly positioned, resulting in water spills. Arvada's varying climate conditions can sometimes cause expansion and contraction in refrigerator components, potentially leading to misalignment or damage.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Defrost Drain: We check for clogs that could cause water to back up and leak.

  • Drain Pan: We examine for cracks or misalignment.
  • Door Seals: We assess door seals for damage or wear that could cause condensation and leaks.
  • Ice Maker Assembly: We check for leaks in the ice maker, which can sometimes lead to water pooling.
