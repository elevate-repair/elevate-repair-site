Why Your Dryer Is Making Loud Noise in Arvada

Living in Arvada, you might experience your dryer making loud noise due to various reasons. One common cause is the accumulation of debris in the drum or a malfunctioning drum bearing. The dry climate of the region can lead to quicker wear and tear on dryer components, increasing the likelihood of such issues.

Another reason for loud noises could be a faulty motor or worn-out belt. In Arvada, where homes often have spacious laundry areas, vibrations can become more noticeable. Ensuring the dryer is on a level surface is crucial to reducing such noise. Additionally, lint buildup can trap moisture, causing metal parts to rust, which may result in unusual sounds.

Lastly, the common practice of drying heavy items like blankets can strain the dryer over time. This extra stress might lead to parts loosening and causing loud, unexpected noises. Regular maintenance and checking for wear can help mitigate these issues.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Drum Bearings — These are checked for wear and tear that commonly cause rumbling noises.
  • Drive Belt — Technicians inspect this for any signs of fraying or damage that can lead to squealing sounds.
  • Motor — The motor is tested for proper functionality to rule out humming or grinding noises.
  • Idler Pulley — We ensure this component is properly aligned and lubricated to prevent squeaking.
  • Roller Wheels — These are examined to ensure they rotate smoothly and do not contribute to thumping noises.
  • Blower Wheel — Checked for obstructions or damage that could lead to rattling.
