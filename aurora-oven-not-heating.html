Why Your Oven Is Not Heating in Aurora

Ovens not heating is a common issue many homeowners in Aurora face, especially during the colder months when appliances can be more heavily relied upon. A variety of technical issues can cause this problem, from electrical malfunctions to faulty components. Understanding these causes can help in addressing the problem effectively.

One common reason your oven might not heat is a malfunctioning heating element. The heating element is responsible for generating the necessary heat, and if it's faulty, your oven will not reach the desired temperature. In Aurora's dry climate, fluctuating temperatures can sometimes exacerbate wear on these components.

Another possibility is an issue with the thermostat. If the thermostat is not accurately reading the internal temperature of the oven, it may not trigger the heating element when needed. Homes in Aurora with older electrical systems might also experience power issues that can affect the oven’s ability to heat properly.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Heating Element — Our technicians will inspect the heating element for signs of damage or wear.
  • Thermostat — We check the thermostat to ensure it's functioning correctly and accurately.
  • Igniter — For gas ovens, the igniter is crucial for starting the oven. A faulty igniter can prevent the oven from heating.
  • Temperature Sensor — We ensure the sensor correctly measures the oven’s internal temperature.
  • Electrical Connections — Loose or damaged wiring can prevent the oven from heating properly.
  • Control Board — The control board manages all the oven's functions, and if it's defective, the oven may not heat.
