Why Your Oven Is Not Heating in Aurora

Experiencing an oven that won't heat can be frustrating, especially in Aurora where home cooking is often a cherished activity. Several common issues could be causing this problem. A faulty thermostat is often the culprit, preventing your oven from reaching the desired temperature. This can be exacerbated by Aurora’s variable climate, which can affect home energy efficiency.

Another potential issue is the igniter. If it fails, the oven may not heat at all. In Aurora, where gas and electric ovens are both popular, a malfunctioning igniter could be a common issue. Additionally, a defective heating element might be to blame, which is common in many aging appliances found in local homes.

Moreover, electrical problems, such as a tripped circuit breaker, can also result in an oven not heating. Aurora residents should check their home’s electrical panel to ensure all circuits are in working order. Ensuring your home's power supply is consistent can often solve the issue.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Thermostat: We verify that it accurately reads the oven's temperature to ensure proper heating.
  • Igniter: We check for any faults or signs of wear that may prevent it from igniting the oven.
  • Heating Element: Our experts inspect it for continuity and functionality.
  • Electrical Connections: We ensure all connections are secure and circuits are live.
  • Control Board: We examine this crucial component for any errors affecting the oven's performance.
  • Gas Supply (if applicable): We confirm that the gas supply is steady and sufficient for ovens using gas.
