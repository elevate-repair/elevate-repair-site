Why Your Oven Is Not Heating in Lakewood

Ovens can malfunction for several reasons, especially in residential areas like Lakewood where seasonal temperature shifts are common. A common culprit is a faulty heating element, which is responsible for generating the heat needed for cooking. When the heating element burns out, your oven will no longer heat efficiently, if at all.

Another issue might be with the oven's thermostat. In Lakewood's fluctuating climate, thermostats can become inaccurate over time, leading to incorrect temperature readings that prevent the oven from heating properly. Additionally, electrical issues such as a blown fuse or a faulty wiring connection can also prevent your oven from heating up.

Furthermore, the gas supply to your oven could be disrupted. If you have a gas oven, any issues with the gas line or any blockages can impede the flow of gas, preventing your oven from heating. Regular maintenance is essential to avoid these common issues and ensure your oven remains in good working condition.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Heating Element: We check for any visible damage or burns that could indicate a malfunction.
  • Thermostat: The thermostat is tested for accuracy to ensure your oven heats to the correct temperature.
  • Igniter: Our technicians ensure the igniter is functioning properly, especially in gas ovens.
  • Control Board: We inspect the control board for any signs of electrical failure or damage.
  • Gas Supply: For gas ovens, we verify that the gas supply is uninterrupted and properly regulated.
  • Wiring and Connections: Any loose or damaged wiring is identified and repaired to restore full functionality.
