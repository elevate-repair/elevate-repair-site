Why Your Washer Is Leaking Water in Denver

Denver homeowners often face the frustrating issue of a leaking washer, which can stem from several common causes. One frequent culprit is a worn-out door seal or gasket, which can fail due to wear and tear over time. The high altitude and dry climate of Denver might contribute to the accelerated degradation of rubber-based components, exacerbating this issue.

Another cause could be a malfunctioning water inlet valve. This component regulates water flow into the washer and may develop leaks if it becomes clogged or damaged. Additionally, improper leveling of the washer, which is common in homes with older foundations typical in some Denver neighborhoods, can lead to leaks as the machine vibrates during operation.

Finally, a cracked or loose hose connection inside the machine can also result in water leakage. Denver's diverse range of home ages and styles means that plumbing layouts can vary significantly, sometimes complicating the repair process. Identifying the exact cause requires a thorough inspection by a professional.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Door Seal: We check for wear and cracking that can cause leaks.
  • Water Inlet Valve: Inspected for clogs or damage that might lead to water overflow.
  • Drain Hose: Examined for any loose connections or cracks.
  • Water Pump: Checked for leaks and proper operation.
  • Drum Bearings: Inspected for wear, which can indirectly cause leaks.
  • Leveling Legs: Ensured they are adjusted correctly to prevent movement and leaks.
