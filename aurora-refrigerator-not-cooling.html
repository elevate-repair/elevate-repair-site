Why Your Refrigerator Is Not Cooling in Aurora

Refrigerators can stop cooling for several reasons, impacting the comfort and convenience of your Aurora home. A common issue is a malfunctioning thermostat, which can prevent the refrigerator from maintaining the correct temperature. Additionally, dirty condenser coils can obstruct heat dissipation, leading to cooling issues.

The climate in Aurora, with its warm summers, can sometimes exacerbate these problems, as refrigerators work harder to maintain cool temperatures. Poor ventilation or blocked vents can also reduce cooling efficiency, a problem often unnoticed in homes across Aurora.

Another frequent cause is a faulty evaporator fan motor, which is crucial for circulating cold air throughout the appliance. Without this circulation, your refrigerator may not cool evenly, leaving some sections warmer than others.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Condenser Coils: We check for dust and debris that could impede cooling.
  • Evaporator Fan Motor: Essential for air circulation, we ensure it's operational.
  • Thermostat: We verify it accurately regulates the refrigerator's temperature.
  • Door Seals: Inspecting for leaks that might let warm air in.
  • Compressor: Ensuring it's running efficiently to cool the refrigerator.
  • Vents and Airflow: We assess for blockages that could affect cooling performance.
