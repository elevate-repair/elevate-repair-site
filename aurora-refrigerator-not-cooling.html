Why Your Refrigerator Is Not Cooling in Aurora

When your refrigerator stops cooling, it disrupts daily life, presenting a significant inconvenience. Common causes include a malfunctioning compressor, a faulty thermostat, or issues with the evaporator fan. Each of these components plays a crucial role in maintaining a consistent temperature. In Aurora, where seasonal temperature swings can be extreme, a well-functioning refrigerator is essential to keep food fresh and safe.

Another reason for a refrigerator not cooling effectively can be due to clogged condenser coils. These coils, located at the back or bottom of the appliance, can accumulate dust and debris over time, particularly in homes with pets or in areas with high dust levels typical of Aurora's semi-arid climate. Ensuring these coils are clean is vital for efficient cooling.

Furthermore, the door seals or gaskets can often be overlooked. If they become brittle or damaged, warm air can enter the fridge, causing fluctuations in temperature. This issue is prevalent in Aurora's older homes, where seals may have degraded over time. Regular inspection and maintenance can prevent such problems, ensuring your refrigerator operates efficiently year-round.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Compressor: Checks for any signs of wear or electrical faults that might impede cooling.
  • Condenser Coils: Inspects and cleans to ensure they are free of dust and debris.
  • Thermostat: Tests to ensure it accurately regulates the internal temperature.
  • Evaporator Fan: Verifies functionality as it circulates cold air throughout the refrigerator.
  • Door Seals: Examines for any cracks or gaps that could allow warm air in.
  • Defrost System: Ensures the system prevents frost buildup which can impair cooling.
