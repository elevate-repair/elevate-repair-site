Dryer Won't Start in Denver?

You press the start button and nothing happens. No tumbling, no heat, no response at all. A dryer that refuses to start can be caused by several electrical and mechanical failures. Our Denver technicians pinpoint the exact cause and get your dryer running again the same day.

Why This Happens

The most common reason a dryer will not start is a failed door switch. Every dryer has a small plunger-activated switch in the door frame that tells the control board the door is securely closed. When this switch fails mechanically or electrically, the dryer receives no signal that the door is shut, and the control board will not allow the motor to engage. You can sometimes hear a clicking sound when pressing the switch manually, but a lack of continuity on a multimeter test confirms the switch has failed internally.

A blown thermal fuse is another leading cause. The thermal fuse is a one-time safety device typically located on the blower housing or exhaust duct. If the dryer overheated during a previous cycle, this fuse blows to prevent a fire hazard. On many dryer models, a blown thermal fuse cuts power to the entire machine, not just the heating circuit, which means the dryer will not start at all. The fuse cannot be reset and must be replaced, and the root cause of the overheat should be investigated to prevent recurrence.

Less common but equally problematic causes include a defective start switch, a failed drive motor, or a faulty electronic control board. The start switch is a momentary-contact switch that sends voltage to the motor when you push the start button. If the internal contacts are worn or corroded, no power reaches the motor. On belt-drive dryers, some models include a belt switch that disables the motor when the drum belt breaks, preventing the motor from spinning freely without a load. A failed control board can also prevent startup if the relay that powers the motor circuit has burned out.

Signs You Need Professional Repair

  • Pressing the start button produces no response whatsoever from the dryer
  • The dryer hums briefly when you press start but the drum does not turn
  • Interior light works but the dryer will not start a cycle
  • The door latch clicks but the dryer does not recognize the door as closed
  • Dryer trips the circuit breaker immediately when you attempt to start it

How We Fix It

Our technicians begin by verifying that the dryer is receiving proper voltage at the outlet. Electric dryers require 240 volts across two legs, and a loss of one leg can prevent startup entirely. We then systematically test the door switch, thermal fuse, start switch, and drive motor for electrical continuity. For dryers with electronic controls, we inspect the control board for visible burn marks on relays and test the output signals during a start attempt.

Once the failed component is identified, we replace it with an OEM-equivalent part designed for your specific dryer model. If the thermal fuse was blown, we also inspect the venting system and cycling thermostat to address the underlying overheat condition. For motor failures, we verify the drum rotates freely by hand before installing the new motor to rule out a seized bearing or drum obstruction that could damage the replacement.
