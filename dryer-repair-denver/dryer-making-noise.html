Dryer Making Noise in Denver?

Squealing, thumping, grinding, or rattling coming from your dryer is more than just annoying. These sounds are warning signs that internal components are wearing out and need attention before they cause a complete breakdown. Our Denver technicians identify the source and replace worn parts the same day.

Why This Happens

Most dryer noises originate from the drum support system. The drum in a standard dryer rides on two or more rubber-coated drum rollers mounted on the rear bulkhead, and in some models, on the front as well. Over years of use, the rubber coating on these rollers wears flat, develops flat spots, or hardens from heat exposure. When this happens, the drum no longer glides smoothly and produces a rhythmic thumping or rumbling sound that corresponds to the drum's rotation speed. The roller shafts can also wear, causing the rollers to wobble and create a scraping noise against the drum.

A worn or frayed drum belt is another common noise source. The belt wraps around the outside of the drum and loops through an idler pulley and the motor pulley to drive rotation. As the belt ages, it can develop cracks, fraying, or glazing on its surface. A glazed belt may slip on the drum and produce a high-pitched squealing sound, especially when the dryer is loaded with heavy items. The idler pulley itself has a bearing that wears over time, and a failing idler pulley creates a steady squeaking or squealing that is present throughout the entire cycle regardless of load size.

Drum glides or slides, which are plastic or felt pads that support the front edge of the drum inside the cabinet, also wear down and cause noise. When these pads wear through, metal-on-metal contact between the drum and the front bulkhead produces a loud scraping or grinding sound. Additionally, the blower wheel that pulls air through the dryer can become obstructed by small items like coins, buttons, or lint clumps that have passed through the lint trap. An obstructed blower creates a rattling, clicking, or fluttering noise that may come and go during the cycle.

Signs You Need Professional Repair

  • Loud thumping or rumbling that follows the rhythm of the drum rotation
  • High-pitched squealing or squeaking that persists throughout the cycle
  • Metal-on-metal scraping or grinding sound when the dryer is running
  • Rattling or clicking noise that seems to come from behind or below the drum
  • Noises that are getting progressively louder over the past several loads

How We Fix It

Our technician will run your dryer briefly to listen to the noise and pinpoint its location within the machine. The type of sound, its timing relative to drum rotation, and where it is loudest in the cabinet all tell us which component is failing. We then disassemble the dryer to access the drum support components, belt, idler pulley, and blower wheel for visual inspection. Worn drum rollers are easy to identify because the rubber will show visible flat spots, cracks, or hardening, and the shafts may have play in them.

When replacing drum rollers, we always replace them as a complete set rather than individually. Replacing just one roller leaves the others at different wear levels, which can cause uneven drum rotation and lead to a return visit. The same principle applies to drum glides. If the belt shows signs of cracking or glazing, we replace it along with the idler pulley, since these components wear at similar rates and a new belt on an old pulley often leads to noise returning within a few months. For blower wheel obstructions, we remove the debris and inspect the fan for cracked or broken fins that could reduce airflow.
