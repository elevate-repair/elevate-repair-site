Dryer Won't Tumble in Denver?

Your dryer turns on and you can hear the motor running, but the drum sits completely still. Without tumbling, clothes pile up at the bottom and never dry. This problem is usually mechanical and very repairable. Our Denver technicians carry the most common replacement parts on every service call for a fast fix.

Why This Happens

The most frequent cause of a dryer that runs but will not tumble is a broken drive belt. The drive belt is a long, thin, ribbed belt that wraps around the entire circumference of the drum, threads through an idler pulley for tension, and connects to the motor pulley. This belt is what transfers the motor's rotational force to the drum. Over time, the belt stretches, dries out from heat exposure, and eventually snaps. When the belt breaks, the motor continues to run and the blower still operates, so you may hear the dryer running and feel warm air, but the drum does not move at all.

Worn drum support rollers can also prevent tumbling, though this usually happens gradually. As the rollers deteriorate, the drum requires more force to turn. Eventually, the resistance becomes too great for the motor and belt to overcome, and the drum stalls. You may notice that the dryer was making increasingly loud thumping noises in the days or weeks before the drum stopped spinning entirely. In severe cases, the roller shafts can seize completely, locking the drum in place.

A failed idler pulley can cause the belt to lose tension and slip off the motor pulley, which stops drum rotation even though the belt itself is intact. The idler pulley spring or bracket can also break, releasing tension entirely. Motor problems are less common but do occur, particularly in older dryers. A motor with a failed start winding may hum but not generate enough torque to turn the drum. In some cases, a seized rear drum bearing creates so much friction that the motor cannot rotate the drum and may trip the motor's thermal overload protector, causing the dryer to shut off intermittently.

Signs You Need Professional Repair

  • The motor hums and the dryer produces heat but the drum does not spin
  • You can rotate the drum easily by hand, indicating the belt has broken
  • The drum barely turns or stalls when you add a load of wet clothes
  • A loud snap or pop sound preceded the drum stopping mid-cycle
  • The dryer was making increasingly loud noises before the drum stopped turning

How We Fix It

Diagnosing a non-tumbling dryer is straightforward for an experienced technician. We first open the dryer cabinet and check the drive belt. If the belt has snapped, we can see it immediately. If the belt is intact, we check its tension by examining the idler pulley assembly for broken springs, a seized pulley bearing, or a cracked mounting bracket. We then spin the drum by hand to evaluate the resistance from the drum rollers and rear bearing. Excessive resistance or grinding indicates that support components have failed.

For a broken belt, we route a new belt around the drum, thread it through the idler pulley, and loop it onto the motor pulley. We always inspect the idler pulley bearing and drum rollers at the same time, because these components wear at similar rates and a new belt on worn rollers can lead to premature belt failure. If the drum rollers need replacement, we swap the full set to ensure balanced support. For a seized drum bearing, we remove the drum to access and replace the bearing assembly at the rear bulkhead.
