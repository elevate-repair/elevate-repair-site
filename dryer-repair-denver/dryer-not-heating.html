Dryer Not Heating in Denver?

Clothes coming out damp after a full cycle? A dryer that runs but produces no heat is one of the most common appliance problems we fix. Our Denver technicians diagnose and repair heating failures same-day so you can get back to your laundry.

Why This Happens

The most common reason a dryer stops heating is a burned-out heating element. In electric dryers, the heating element is a coiled wire inside a metal housing that glows red-hot during operation. Over time, the coil develops weak spots from repeated thermal expansion and contraction, eventually breaking and creating an open circuit. When this happens, the dryer tumbles normally but blows only room-temperature air. A multimeter test across the element terminals will confirm whether continuity has been lost.

Another frequent culprit is a blown thermal fuse. This one-time-use safety device is mounted on the exhaust duct or blower housing and is designed to trip if the dryer overheats. Once the thermal fuse blows, it cuts power to the heating circuit entirely. While replacing the fuse itself is straightforward, the underlying cause of the overheat must also be addressed, often a clogged vent or failed cycling thermostat, or the new fuse will simply blow again.

For gas dryers, a defective gas valve coil or faulty igniter is usually to blame. The igniter glows to reach ignition temperature, then the gas valve coils open to release gas. If the igniter is weak, it may glow but never reach the threshold needed to trigger the valve. Worn gas valve coils can also fail intermittently, heating for part of the cycle before shutting off. Additionally, a malfunctioning cycling thermostat or timer with burned contacts can prevent the heating circuit from engaging at the right times during the cycle.

Signs You Need Professional Repair

  • Clothes are still damp or cold after a complete drying cycle
  • The dryer drum spins and the motor runs but there is no warm air inside
  • Gas dryer igniter glows briefly but the burner never lights
  • Dryer heats for a few minutes then stops producing heat mid-cycle
  • You notice a burning smell or the thermal fuse has tripped repeatedly

How We Fix It

Our technicians start with a systematic diagnosis. We check the thermal fuse for continuity, test the heating element or gas igniter with a multimeter, inspect the cycling thermostat, and verify that the high-limit thermostat is functioning correctly. For gas models, we also test the gas valve solenoid coils and confirm proper igniter current draw. This methodical approach ensures we identify the root cause rather than just replacing parts blindly.

Once diagnosed, we carry out the repair using manufacturer-specified replacement parts. If the heating element has failed, we install a new element rated for your specific dryer model. If a thermal fuse has blown, we replace the fuse and inspect the entire exhaust path to prevent future overheating. For gas dryers with igniter or valve coil issues, we replace the worn components and verify proper flame ignition through a complete test cycle.
