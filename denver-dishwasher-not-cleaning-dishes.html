Why Your Dishwasher Is Not Cleaning Dishes in Denver

In Denver, dishwashers often face unique challenges due to the city's hard water, which can lead to mineral buildup and reduce cleaning efficiency. This mineral accumulation might obstruct spray arms or clog filters, hindering their ability to clean dishes properly. Additionally, the high altitude can sometimes affect the appliance's water pressure, impacting overall performance.

Another common issue is the improper loading of dishes, which can block the spray arms. Denver's varied climate might also cause seals and gaskets to wear out faster, resulting in water leakage and ineffective cleaning cycles. Regular maintenance and correct usage are essential to keep your dishwasher operating at peak performance.

Detergent selection plays a crucial role as well. With Denver's specific water conditions, using the wrong type of detergent can result in residue on your dishes. Choosing a detergent formulated for hard water can significantly improve your dishwasher's cleaning ability.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Spray Arms — Ensuring they are free from clogs and can rotate fully.
  • Filters — Checking for blockages that may reduce water flow.
  • Water Inlet Valve — Making sure it allows the correct amount of water into the dishwasher.
  • Heating Element — Confirming it's functioning to properly dry your dishes.
  • Detergent Dispenser — Ensuring it opens at the right time during the wash cycle.
  • Door Gaskets — Checking for wear that could cause leaks and reduce cleaning efficiency.
