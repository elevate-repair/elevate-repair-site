Why Your Dishwasher Is Not Draining in Aurora

In Aurora, the causes of a dishwasher not draining can range from simple blockages to more complex mechanical failures. One common culprit is a clogged filter, which can occur due to hard water deposits or food debris, both prevalent in homes throughout the area. Additionally, the drain hose might be kinked or obstructed, especially in older homes with less modern kitchen layouts.

The climate in Aurora can also play a role. Cold winters may affect the plumbing systems, leading to issues with water flow and drainage. Furthermore, mineral buildup, often seen in areas with hard water, can lead to blockages that prevent your dishwasher from draining properly. Regular maintenance and inspections can help mitigate these issues.

Another potential cause is a malfunctioning drain pump, which may fail due to wear and tear or electrical issues. This requires professional examination to determine the extent of the damage and the best course of action. Regardless of the cause, it's crucial to address these problems promptly to avoid further damage to your appliance and your home.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Filter: We check for clogs and clean out any debris that may be impeding water flow.
  • Drain Hose: Our technicians ensure there are no kinks or blockages in the hose that could prevent proper drainage.
  • Air Gap: We inspect the air gap for blockages, a common issue in Aurora due to mineral buildup.
  • Drain Pump: We test the pump to ensure it’s functioning correctly and not hindering water drainage.
  • Garbage Disposal Connection: Our team checks the connection to the garbage disposal for clogs or improper installation.
  • Float Switch: We examine the float switch to ensure it’s not stuck in the wrong position, preventing the dishwasher from draining.
