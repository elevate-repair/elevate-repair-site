Why Your Refrigerator Is Not Running in Aurora

Refrigerators are an essential appliance in every Aurora home, and when they stop running, it can lead to food spoilage and inconvenience. One common cause of a refrigerator not running is an issue with the power supply. In some Aurora homes, older wiring or circuit breaker problems can lead to power disruptions that affect the operation of your refrigerator.

Another frequent cause is a faulty compressor. The compressor is the heart of your refrigerator's cooling system, and if it fails, your refrigerator will not cool properly or may stop running altogether. In Aurora's varying climate, refrigerators can sometimes struggle to maintain optimal performance, particularly during seasonal temperature swings.

Additionally, issues with the thermostat or temperature control board can prevent your refrigerator from operating. These components ensure that your appliance maintains the right temperature, and any malfunctions can disrupt the cooling process. Given Aurora’s occasional temperature fluctuations, it's crucial to have these components in top condition.

Refrigerator Repair Considerations for Aurora Homes

Aurora's summer temperatures regularly reach the mid-90s, which places substantial stress on residential refrigerator compressors—especially in units installed in kitchens with south- or west-facing sun exposure and limited ventilation clearance. French-door and side-by-side models from Samsung and LG, common builder choices in Aurora's 2000s-era housing developments, show elevated compressor failure rates when ambient kitchen temperatures run consistently high.

Ice maker problems are the single most common refrigerator service call in Aurora. The city's moderately hard municipal water leaves calcium and magnesium deposits in ice maker water lines, inlet valves, and molds. A refrigerator that makes small, hollow, or misshapen cubes—or stops making ice entirely—often needs a descaling and valve inspection rather than full component replacement. Our technicians confirm water hardness contribution before recommending parts.

Aurora's large footprint and housing density mean many refrigerators were installed in tight kitchen enclosures without adequate rear clearance, particularly in townhomes and paired homes in developments like Murphy Creek and Harvest Crossing. Insufficient rear clearance forces the compressor to work harder, shortens its service life, and causes the refrigerator to run warm. We check clearance and condenser coil condition on every service call.
