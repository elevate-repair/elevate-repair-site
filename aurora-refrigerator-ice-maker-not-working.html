Why Your Refrigerator Is Ice Maker Not Working in Aurora

Refrigerators are essential in any Aurora home, and when the ice maker stops working, it can disrupt your daily routine. Several common factors can cause ice makers to malfunction, and understanding these can help in addressing the issue promptly. In Aurora, where temperature variations can be quite significant, these factors may be influenced by local conditions.

One typical cause of a non-functional ice maker is a clogged water filter. Over time, mineral deposits from Aurora's water supply can accumulate, leading to reduced water flow. Additionally, a malfunctioning water inlet valve can prevent water from reaching the ice maker. Another common issue is a faulty ice maker mold thermostat, which may prevent the machine from cycling properly to produce ice.

Electrical problems, such as wiring issues or a defective motor, can also lead to an ice maker not working. In some cases, the ice maker's arm may be stuck in the off position, inhibiting ice production. With these potential issues in mind, it’s crucial to have a professional like Elevate Repair examine and diagnose the problem accurately.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Water Inlet Valve: Ensures water flows into the ice maker.
  • Water Filter: Examines for clogs that could reduce water flow.
  • Ice Maker Mold Thermostat: Checks if it's regulating temperature properly.
  • Motor and Gears: Inspects for any signs of wear or electrical failure.
  • Control Module: Assesses if it's sending correct signals to start ice production.
  • Ice Maker Arm: Verifies that it’s not stuck in the off position.

Refrigerator Repair Considerations for Aurora Homes

Aurora's moderately hard municipal water is the dominant cause of ice maker service calls in the city. Calcium and magnesium deposits build up inside the water inlet valve screen, fill tube, and ice mold ejector over time—producing small, hollow, or misshapen cubes before production stops entirely. In Aurora's Anschutz/Fitzsimons corridor and Saddle Rock communities, water hardness accelerates this buildup faster than average. We descale or replace the inlet valve before ordering ice maker assembly parts on every Aurora call.

Ice maker water line freeze-ups are a recurring issue in Aurora refrigerators installed against exterior walls, especially in townhomes and detached homes near the eastern edge of the city along E-470. During Front Range cold snaps, water supply lines routed through exterior walls can freeze at the wall penetration—preventing water from reaching the ice maker while the rest of the refrigerator functions normally. We check water flow at the inlet valve and trace the supply line path before diagnosing the ice maker module itself.

Aurora's large stock of builder-installed French-door and side-by-side refrigerators from the late 2000s includes many Samsung and LG models whose ice makers sit inside the freezer door or upper compartment. These designs place the ice maker where defrost system failures cause frost to accumulate on the auger or optical sensors—preventing ice ejection even when the water supply and mold are functioning. We check the defrost system and look for frost at the ice maker location on every Aurora ice-maker call before replacing the module.
