Why Your Oven Won't Turn Off in Denver

Having an oven that won't turn off can be concerning, especially when you consider the implications of leaving it running unattended. One common cause is a faulty thermostat, which may inaccurately sense the oven's temperature, instructing it to continue heating even when it should shut off. The dry climate in Denver can potentially affect the wear and tear on these components over time.

Another possibility is a malfunctioning control board. The control board acts as the oven's brain, dictating all operations. If it malfunctions, it could fail to send the signal to stop heating. In Denver's varied climate, electrical components can occasionally wear out faster, contributing to these types of issues.

Additionally, a stuck relay or a shorted wire could also be the culprits. These issues can cause continuous power flow to the heating element, making the oven unable to shut down. Considering Denver's fluctuating temperatures, these components might experience increased stress, leading to frequent repairs.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Thermostat: Ensures accurate temperature readings and proper shut-off signals.
  • Control Board: Manages all oven functions; inspected for faults or damage.
  • Door Switch: Checks if the oven door switch is functioning correctly, as it can affect the oven's operation.
  • Heating Element: Inspected for continuous power supply issues or damage.
  • Wiring: Evaluated for any shorts or damage that might cause operational problems.
  • Relays: Examined to confirm they open and close circuits correctly.
