Washer Repair Considerations for Highlands Ranch Homes

Vibration during spin is the most frequent washer complaint from Highlands Ranch homeowners with second-floor laundry rooms. Worn shock absorbers allow the drum to rock during the spin cycle, transmitting force directly to the floor structure. The vibration is often loud enough to be heard throughout the home. We check shock absorbers, bearing condition, and leveling on every second-floor Highlands Ranch not-spinning or vibration call.

Drum bearings on Highlands Ranch's builder-installed LG, Samsung, and Whirlpool front-loads typically last 8–12 years. When Highlands Ranch homeowners report loud grinding or rumbling on spin cycles, bearing replacement is the most probable repair on units in this age range. We confirm the diagnosis by rotating the drum manually to check for roughness or lateral play before quoting the repair.

Stacked unit disassembly is required to replace drum bearings or shock absorbers on Highlands Ranch townhome washers. The repair cannot be completed with the dryer sitting on top of the machine. Our technicians bring strapping tools and cabinet supports to safely separate and support the dryer during the repair, and reassemble and test the stack before leaving.
