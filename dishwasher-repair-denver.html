Dishwasher Repair Services We Offer

We repair built-in, portable, and drawer-style dishwashers from every major manufacturer. Modern dishwashers rely on a combination of mechanical components, electronic controls, and precise water management to clean effectively. When any part of this system fails, dishes come out dirty, water pools where it should not, or the machine refuses to operate at all. Our technicians carry common dishwasher parts including drain pumps, wash motors, water inlet valves, door latches, and heating elements.

Cleaning performance issues often trace back to spray arm problems, a worn wash impeller, or a malfunctioning detergent dispenser. Clogged spray arm nozzles restrict water flow, reducing cleaning power. A cracked or warped spray arm may not spin properly, leaving some areas of the rack untouched. We inspect the wash system from the inlet valve through the spray arms and address each issue we find.

Drainage problems are typically caused by a failed drain pump, a clogged drain hose, or a blocked garbage disposal connection. Standing water in the dishwasher tub creates odors and can damage the tub interior over time. We clear blockages, test drain pump operation, and replace failed components to restore proper drainage. We also check the check valve to prevent backflow from the sink drain.

Leak repairs require careful inspection because water can come from multiple sources: the door gasket, tub seal, pump seals, water inlet valve, or hose connections. We identify the exact leak point and replace the affected part. For electronic issues, we diagnose control board faults, touchpad failures, and sensor malfunctions using error codes and multimeter testing. We service Bosch, Samsung, LG, Whirlpool, KitchenAid, Maytag, GE, Frigidaire, Miele, and all other residential dishwasher brands.
