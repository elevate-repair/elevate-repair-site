Common Samsung Repair Issues

The most well-known Samsung appliance issue involves the ice maker in their French door refrigerators, particularly the RF28 family and related models. These units frequently experience ice buildup behind the ice maker housing, which causes the fan to freeze over and the compartment to stop producing ice. Samsung issued a service bulletin and a retrofit kit for some affected models, but many units in Denver homes still have the original components. Our technicians install the updated parts and ensure proper airflow is restored.

Samsung front-load washers can develop issues with the spider arm assembly that connects the drum to the rear bearing. The spider arm is made from a cast aluminum alloy that corrodes over time due to contact with detergent and moisture. When the spider arm cracks, the drum wobbles during spin cycles and produces a loud banging sound. This is a significant repair, but it extends the life of the machine considerably. We also frequently replace door boot seals on Samsung front-loaders that have developed tears or persistent mold growth.

Samsung dryers are generally reliable, but the moisture sensor bars inside the drum can become coated with dryer sheet residue, causing the sensor dry cycles to end prematurely and leave clothes damp. Cleaning or replacing these sensors resolves the issue. On gas Samsung dryers, the gas valve coils can weaken over time, producing intermittent heating that makes drying cycles take two or three times longer than normal.

Samsung dishwashers, including those with the WaterWall linear wash system, may develop issues with the wash arm motor or the drain pump. Error codes like 5C or 5E indicate drainage problems, often caused by a clogged drain hose or a failed pump impeller. Samsung ranges with Flex Duo dividers can experience failures in the divider motor or the upper and lower heating elements, which we repair on-site.
