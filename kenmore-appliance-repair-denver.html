Common Kenmore Repair Issues

The biggest challenge with Kenmore appliance repair is parts sourcing. Since Sears closed most of its retail locations, finding genuine Kenmore replacement parts has become more difficult for many homeowners. However, most Kenmore appliances are rebranded versions of appliances made by Whirlpool, LG, or Electrolux. The first three digits of the Kenmore model number identify the original manufacturer: models starting with 110 are made by Whirlpool, 795 by LG, and 417 by Electrolux. Our technicians use this knowledge to source compatible parts from the original manufacturer, which are often more readily available and identical to the Kenmore-branded version.

Kenmore dryers are one of our most common service calls for the brand. Aging thermal fuses are a consistent failure point, particularly on models that are five years or older. The thermal fuse is a safety device that blows when the dryer overheats, and it is commonly triggered by a clogged lint screen, a restricted exhaust vent, or a failing heating element that runs too hot. When the thermal fuse blows, the dryer either stops heating or will not start at all. We replace the thermal fuse and inspect the entire venting path to identify the root cause and prevent a repeat failure.

Kenmore refrigerators, particularly the Elite French door models manufactured by LG, can develop the same linear compressor issues found in LG-branded units. Symptoms include a clicking sound from the compressor area, the refrigerator failing to cool, and error codes on the display. Older Kenmore side-by-side models built by Whirlpool may experience defrost system failures, where the defrost heater or defrost timer stops working and ice builds up on the evaporator coils, blocking airflow and causing the refrigerator compartment to warm up.

Kenmore washers share the same repair patterns as their parent manufacturers. Top-load models built by Whirlpool commonly develop lid switch failures, suspension rod wear, and drive coupler breakage. Front-load models built by LG or Electrolux may have door boot seal leaks or drain pump failures. Kenmore dishwashers and ovens follow similar patterns. Our experience with all the brands behind the Kenmore name allows us to diagnose and repair these appliances efficiently.
