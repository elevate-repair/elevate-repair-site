Stove Burner Not Working Repair in Denver

Whether you have a gas range that won't ignite or an electric cooktop with a dead burner, a non-functioning stove burner disrupts your entire kitchen routine. Our Denver technicians repair all burner types with same-day service.

Why This Happens

On gas ranges, the spark igniter is the most common failure point. Each burner has a small ceramic igniter near the burner base that generates a rapid clicking spark when you turn the knob. Over time, food debris, grease, and moisture accumulate around the igniter electrode and ground wire, preventing the spark from jumping the gap to ignite the gas. In other cases the igniter itself cracks or the wire connection corrodes, eliminating the spark entirely. You may hear the clicking sound without seeing a spark, or the clicking may stop altogether.

Electric ranges and cooktops use radiant surface elements (coil or smooth-top) that can burn out after years of thermal cycling. A coil element develops a break in the resistance wire inside, creating an open circuit that prevents any heat output. On smooth-top ranges, the radiant element beneath the glass can fail in one section, producing uneven heat or no heat at all. The infinite switch that controls each burner's power level is another frequent failure—internal contacts wear out or burn, leaving the switch unable to complete the circuit at any setting.

Clogged burner ports affect gas ranges specifically. The small holes around the burner cap distribute the gas flame evenly. When these ports become blocked by food spills, dried sauces, or carbon buildup, the flame pattern becomes irregular—some sections may light while others remain dark, or the flame may be weak and yellow instead of a strong blue cone. A bad ignition switch at the control knob can also prevent the spark module from receiving the signal to fire, even though the module and igniter are both functional.

Signs You Need Professional Repair

  • A gas burner clicks but never ignites, or produces no clicking sound at all when the knob is turned.
  • An electric coil element does not glow red at any heat setting.
  • The burner heats on some settings but not others, indicating an infinite switch problem.
  • A gas burner flame is yellow, uneven, or only lights on one side of the burner cap.
  • Multiple burners fail simultaneously, suggesting a wiring or control module issue.

How We Fix It

For gas burner issues, our technician begins by cleaning and inspecting the burner cap, ports, and igniter electrode. Many ignition problems are resolved by clearing debris from the burner ports with a specialized tool and realigning the igniter gap. If the igniter is cracked or the electrode is corroded beyond cleaning, we replace it and test the spark intensity. We also inspect the spark module—the electronic unit that powers all igniters—to confirm it delivers the correct voltage to each igniter circuit.

Electric burner repairs start with continuity testing of the surface element. A burned-out coil element is straightforward to replace: we disconnect the terminal block, remove the old element, and install a new one matched to your range model. For smooth-top ranges, we access the radiant element beneath the glass and test it for proper resistance. Infinite switch failures are confirmed by checking for voltage output at each heat position, and a failed switch is replaced with an OEM part.
