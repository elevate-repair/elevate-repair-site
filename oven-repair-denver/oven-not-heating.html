Oven Not Heating Repair in Denver

An oven that refuses to heat is one of the most common appliance failures we see in Denver homes. Whether your oven stays completely cold or barely warms up, our certified technicians diagnose and repair the root cause the same day you call.

Why This Happens

In electric ovens, the most frequent cause of a no-heat condition is a burned-out bake or broil element. These coiled heating elements operate at extremely high temperatures and eventually develop visible cracks, blisters, or burn-through spots that break the electrical circuit. When the bake element fails, the oven cavity receives no heat from below; when the broil element fails, top-down heating is lost entirely. In either case the oven may appear to turn on—the control panel lights up and the timer runs—but no actual heat is produced.

Gas ovens rely on an igniter to reach a high enough temperature to open the gas safety valve. Over time the igniter weakens and can no longer draw enough current to trip the valve, so gas never flows to the burner. You may notice the igniter glowing orange for several minutes without the burner lighting, or it may not glow at all. A completely failed gas safety valve can also prevent gas flow even when the igniter is functioning properly, though this is less common.

Beyond elements and igniters, a defective oven temperature sensor (thermistor) can send incorrect resistance readings to the control board, preventing the oven from activating the heating circuit. The control board itself may also be at fault—a failed relay on the board can cut power to the element or igniter circuit. Our technicians use multimeter testing to isolate which component in the chain has failed before recommending a repair.

Signs You Need Professional Repair

  • The oven displays the set temperature but the cavity stays cold after 15 minutes or more.
  • The bake or broil element does not glow red when the oven is turned on.
  • A gas oven igniter glows but the burner never lights, or you smell a faint gas odor.
  • The oven preheats partially but never reaches the target temperature.
  • Error codes such as F1, F3, or F4 appear on the control panel display.

How We Fix It

Our technician begins with a thorough diagnostic, measuring element continuity, igniter amperage draw, sensor resistance, and control board relay output. For a burned-out bake or broil element, we remove the failed part and install an OEM-grade replacement, then verify the oven reaches the correct temperature with a calibrated thermometer. Gas oven igniter replacements involve disconnecting the gas supply, removing the old igniter from the burner assembly, and installing a new igniter rated for your specific model.

If the temperature sensor is faulty, we replace it and recalibrate the oven. Control board issues are confirmed through voltage testing at the relay output before we recommend a board replacement, since boards are among the more expensive components. Throughout the process we test safety devices including thermal fuses and gas valves to ensure your oven operates safely.
