Refrigerator Repair Considerations for Arvada Homes

Power supply issues are the most common cause of a completely silent Arvada refrigerator. In Arvada's pre-1980 homes along the Ralston Creek corridor and western Arvada neighborhoods, kitchen circuits were wired before GFCI requirements and often shared with other loads. A refrigerator on a shared circuit that trips its breaker silently—or that is plugged into an aging GFCI outlet that trips on compressor startup—will appear dead without any internal failure. We verify voltage at the outlet, check all GFCI outlets on the kitchen circuit, and test the main board fuse before opening any access panel on every Arvada refrigerator no-run call.

Start relay failure is the most common single-component cause of a not-running Arvada refrigerator where the lights and display still work. The start relay triggers the compressor on each cooling cycle; when it fails, the control board attempts to start the compressor every few minutes, producing a clicking-then-silence pattern. This is the correct first replacement on Whirlpool, Samsung, and LG models—the most common brands in Arvada's 2000s kitchen remodel stock—because a relay costs a fraction of a compressor. We test the start relay with a resistance check and shake test before condemning the compressor on every Arvada clicking-but-not-running call.

Thermal overload protection trips are a cause of intermittent not-running in Arvada's summer heat. In refrigerators installed in Arvada's tight mid-century kitchen alcoves with inadequate rear clearance, compressors run at elevated discharge temperatures and trip their thermal overload protectors during hot weather. The unit goes silent, cools down over 2–4 hours, restarts, and appears to work—then fails again. Arvada homes with refrigerators in south-facing kitchen alcoves or in converted garage spaces are most prone to this seasonal pattern. We check ambient temperature, clearance, and condenser condition on every Arvada intermittent no-run call before testing internal components.
