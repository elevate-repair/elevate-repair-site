Why Your Dryer Is Not Starting in Lakewood

If your dryer is refusing to start, it can disrupt your daily routine, especially in a bustling place like Lakewood. Common causes include issues with the power supply, malfunctioning door switches, or faulty thermal fuses. Lakewood homes, with their diverse range of appliance models, often face these technical hiccups due to normal wear and tear.

Another reason could be the climate's impact on electrical components. The dryer’s control board might be affected by fluctuation in humidity levels throughout the seasons. Additionally, the lint filter, if not cleaned regularly, can lead to overheating, causing components to fail and preventing the dryer from starting.

Older Lakewood homes might also have outdated electrical setups, which can exacerbate problems. Checking for tripped circuit breakers or blown fuses is a good first step. However, the underlying issue often requires professional inspection and repair to ensure safety and functionality.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Power Supply — Verifying that the dryer is receiving adequate power and the outlet is functioning correctly.
  • Door Switch — Ensuring the door switch engages properly as it acts as a safety mechanism.
  • Thermal Fuse — Checking if the thermal fuse has blown due to overheating, which is a common safety feature.
  • Control Board — Inspecting the electronic control board for signs of damage or failure.
  • Start Switch — Examining the start switch for continuity, as a faulty switch can prevent the dryer from operating.
  • Motor — Assessing the motor for any mechanical issues or failures that could impede operation.

Dryer Repair Considerations for Lakewood Homes

Lakewood's prevailing west winds from the foothills create back-drafting conditions for dryer vents that terminate on the east or north sides of homes—particularly in the Green Mountain and Bear Creek areas where exposure to wind is significant. Back-drafting allows cold outside air and debris to enter the duct system and can cause the dryer's cycling thermostat to trip prematurely, cutting heat before the cycle completes. We inspect vent termination cap condition and airflow direction on every Lakewood dryer service call.

Many Lakewood homes built in the 1950s and 1960s have laundry areas that were added as part of utility room conversions or garage finish-outs. These added spaces sometimes have non-standard duct routing—including vertical risers or horizontal runs through tight joist bays—that restricts airflow significantly. A dryer duct that passes through a cold uninsulated exterior wall section in a Lakewood home can develop ice blockages in winter, cutting off exhaust airflow entirely.

Lakewood's older electrical service panels—particularly in homes built before 1980—may have 30-amp dryer circuits on older wiring that does not meet current gauge standards. Undersized wiring can cause dryers to operate at reduced voltage, resulting in extended dry times that are often attributed to a failing heating element. We verify voltage and amperage at the outlet before diagnosing internal components on Lakewood electric dryer calls.
