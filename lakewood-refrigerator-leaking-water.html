Why Your Refrigerator Is Leaking Water in Lakewood

Refrigerator leaks can be a frustrating problem for any homeowner in Lakewood. One common cause is a clogged or frozen defrost drain. Over time, food particles and other debris can block the drain hose, leading to water pooling at the base of the refrigerator. This is a frequent issue that our technicians encounter, especially in homes where regular maintenance is overlooked.

Another potential culprit is a faulty water inlet valve, which can cause leaks if it becomes loose or damaged. Given Lakewood's varied climate, temperature fluctuations can sometimes exacerbate these issues, causing seals to crack or hoses to loosen. This is why routine checks and maintenance are crucial to preventing leaks.

Additionally, improper leveling of your refrigerator can lead to water leakage. When a unit isn't perfectly level, it can cause water from the evaporator coils to spill over rather than drain away as designed. This is particularly common in older homes where floors may have settled unevenly over time.

What Our Technicians Inspect

  • Defrost Drain: We check for blockages or freezing in the defrost drain, which can cause water to back up and leak.
  • Water Inlet Valve: Inspecting the valve for leaks or damage that may result in water escaping where it shouldn't.
  • Door Seals: Examining the door seals for cracks or gaps that can allow warm air in, causing excess condensation.
  • Drain Pan: Ensuring the drain pan is intact and correctly positioned to catch condensation.
  • Ice Maker: Evaluating the ice maker components for leaks, especially in models with built-in water lines.
  • Refrigerator Leveling: Checking and adjusting the leveling of the refrigerator to prevent water from pooling.
